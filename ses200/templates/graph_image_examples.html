Making Images Accessible

Graph Images

Like generic images, graphs allow us to consume large amounts of information quickly. However, graphs can be tricky. Because their purpose is generally to convey statistical information, care must be taken to ensure that the overall message is clear to the user.

Some solid guidelines for graphs include:

  • Include the title
  • Don't state any conclusions
  • Mention what is being charted
  • State any trends indicated by the graph

A bad example of graph image use

Because our user only has the title available, they could be unable to any useful information from the graph.

A good example of graph image use

By including specific details about the graph in the "alt" tags, our users are able to quickly obtain the gist of the graph displayed.
